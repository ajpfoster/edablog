Foundations Core Reflections

Posted by Ash Foster on May 23rd, 2019

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I've learned that I can be patient with my learning. Often I look for shortcuts but I've really enjoyed the reflection process explored in foundations as it sets a precedent for getting out what you put in. This is a good way to approach life and its problems holistically and my ego has largely been humbled by how well this was presented in curriculum.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Mainly what I said above about the curriculum but also how well the soft skills covered compliment the flipped classroom environment to provide a more personal learning experience.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Probably the reflections which were the parts I believe I gained the most from ironically.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Because web development is technically difficult it is best to tackle problems with a proven methodology or approach. Everyone is different but the core takeaways can be leveraged by an individual as coping strategies for roadblocks in development.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

Definitely not a waste a time as stated above web development is hard as such much of the industry is team focussed. Practicing these soft skills will allow me to be a more effective member of a development team.
