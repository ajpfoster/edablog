IQ vs EQ

Emotional intelligence brings together the fields of emotions and intelligence by viewing emotions as useful sources of information that help one to make sense of and navigate the social environment.

EQ is centred on skills such as:

  • Identifying emotions
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Controlling one's own emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communication
  • Relating to others

IQ represents abilities such as:

  • Visual and spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Working memory and short-term memory
  • Quantitative reasoning

Early academic research by psychologists Mayer and Salovey defined it as "The ability to monitor one's own and others' feelings, to discriminate among them , and to use this information to guide one's thinking and action." (Mayer & Salovey, 1990)

Later this was refined and broken down into four proposed abilities which make up the Four-Branch model of Emotional Intelligence: Perceiving, using, understanding and managing emotions. (Mayer & Salovey, 1997)

IQ is still recognized as an important element of success but today experts recognise it is not the only determinate of life success and perhaps more importantly life satisfaction. IQ should instead be viewed as a narrow segment of a complex array of influences that includes emotional intelligence and other multivariate.
